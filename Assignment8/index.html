Tarot's origins and history

Diving into the tarot canvas be daunting at first. Tarot cards have been since 14th century originally as an entertaining parlor game rather than a divination.
Since 18th century, followers of the occult in France and England started to view the symbolic pictures as having more meaning that could be used as divination tool. Ever since then Tarot became a crucial part of occult philiosophy, similar to Yoga nowadays, it has been a form of divination to represent the divine, higher self.

Types of Cards and Spreads

Created by unique individuals and cultures, each tarot deck with 78 cards has unique characteristics and visual styles. The Rider-Waite tarot deck created in 1910 is one of the most popular tarot decks in use for the purpose

Each deck is categorized into Major Arcana and Minor Arcana. Major Arcana is a jounrey that begins with The Fool and ends with The World. While the cards connect to each other and build upon each other, each card stands alone. Major Arcana uses symbols such as "Emperor" or "Star" that are familiar to people Minor Arcana is more implicit.

There are many types of spread, a method in which a taort reader physically lays out the cards and interprets the meanings for the querent. The most common spreads are "Basic Three Card" and "Celtic Cross Spread."
"Basic Three Card" has only three cards to represent past, present, and future.

How to start

1. Choose your deck and spread of your choice.
2. Have questions or topics in your mind.
Consider what you'd like to know from this reading. It can be a simple question as, "How are my relationships going?" 3. Shuffle and Choose your cards
4. Tune in to your intuition and interpret the chosen cards. According to the spreads you chose, each card will represent different meanings.

While you should use your intutiion as best as possible, you learn as you search online for varied interpretations and details about the cards and their meanings. Find a study buddy to accompany your journey!
